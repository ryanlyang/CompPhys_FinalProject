\begin{abstract}
This paper reports the restart baseline results for the computational physics final project on offline JetClass classification. We reimplemented the preliminary studies with controlled corruption benchmarks, unlabeled shift-metric calibration, and interpretability-based masking analysis. We summarize five-seed aggregate results and identify the most stable diagnostics for subsequent project phases.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}
This manuscript summarizes the first results block after the project restart: a clean offline baseline, corruption stress testing, calibration of unlabeled shift metrics, and interpretability effectiveness under matched masking budgets. The interpretability study includes input gradients, Integrated Gradients \cite{sundararajan2017axiomatic}, and SmoothGrad \cite{smilkov2017smoothgrad}.

\section{Conclusion}
The restart baseline is stable across seeds and provides a reproducible reference point for the next stage of the project. In particular, high clean AUC, consistent corruption-tracking shift metrics, and clear targeted-versus-random masking gaps establish a strong empirical foundation for future training-time interventions.
